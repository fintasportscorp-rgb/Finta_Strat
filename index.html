Understanding Percentile Calculations

💡

Key Insight

Percentiles are calculated separately for each formation. Each formation's matches are compared only against other matches using that same formation.

⚠️

Why Higher Values Can Have Lower Percentiles

Formation Value Percentile
3-4-3 314 63rd
4-2-3-1 319 63rd
3-5-2 304 68th

3-5-2 has the lowest value but highest percentile because 304 is exceptional for a 3-5-2!

🎯

What This Tells You

High percentile = Formation is overperforming

Low percentile = Formation is underperforming

More tactically useful than comparing raw numbers across different systems.

Formation Fluidity Guide

⚽

Understanding Formation Fluidity

Formation labels (4-2-3-1, 3-5-2, etc.) are SNAPSHOTS of fluid tactical systems.

•Teams constantly shift between shapes based on phase of play
•Player positioning and movement create dynamic variations
•Tactical instructions adapt formations in real-time
•Game situation and scoreline influence shape changes
💡This tool helps you understand which formations perform best against specific opponents.
🔄

Example: 4-1-4-1 → 4-2-3-1

A common transformation during possession phase:

4-1-4-1 (Defensive)
      ST
  LW  CM  CM  RW
      CDM
  LB  CB  CB  RB
→
4-2-3-1 (Possession)
      ST
  LW    CAM    RW
    CDM    CDM
  LB  CB  CB  RB
↑ Higher touches in midfield
↑ More progressive passes
↑ Better pass completion %
⚡

Example: 4-2-3-1 → 3-4-3 Hybrid

RB inverts to midfield, LB pushes high as wide attacker:

4-2-3-1 (Standard)
      ST
  LW    CAM    RW
    CDM    CDM
  LB  CB  CB  RB
→
3-4-3 (Attacking)
  LB    ST    RW
CDM  RB  CDM  CAM
    CB  CB  CB
Overload Created:Left flank 2v1 advantage, 4-man midfield control
📊

Overload & Underload Zones

Overload Zones

Areas where you have MORE players than the opponent.

4-3-3 vs 4-2-3-1: 3 CMs vs 2 CDMs = 3v2 midfield advantage

Underload Zones

Areas where you have FEWER players than the opponent.

4-3-3 vs 4-4-2: 1 ST vs 2 CBs = isolated striker
🎯Use this tool to find formations that create overloads where you need them!
📈

Reading the Statistics

Statistical patterns reveal tactical characteristics:

High touches_mid_3rd→ Midfield overload / control
High carries_into_final_third→ Wide attacking overloads
High progressive_passes→ Effective build-up play
Low tackles_mid_3rd→ Weak midfield presence
Tip: Hover over metric headers to see explanations!
